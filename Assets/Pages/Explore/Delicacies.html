Buko Pie

Buko Pie is a delightful baked delicacy hailing from the Philippines, renowned for its unique blend of flavors and textures. This pie features tender, young coconut meat enveloped in a rich, creamy filling, all encased within a crisp, buttery pastry crust. Its perfect balance of sweetness and texture makes it an ideal choice for a satisfying snack or a delicious dessert. Whether enjoyed warm or cold, Buko Pie offers a taste of tropical paradise with every bite.

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon fine salt
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 3/4 cup shortening
  • 1/4 cup cold butter
  • 1/2 cup ice water
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 can (300ml) sweetened condensed milk
  • 5-6 cups sliced young coconut meat
  • 1 cup coconut water
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 small egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

Procedures

Prepare the Crust:

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: Combine 3 ½ cups of all-purpose flour and 1 teaspoon of fine salt in a bowl. Use a balloon whisk to mix them together.
  2. Add Fats: Add ¼ cup cold butter and ¾ cup shortening to the flour mixture. Use a pastry mixer to blend them until the mixture becomes crumbly.
  3. Add Water: Gradually add ½ cup ice water, one tablespoon at a time, while continuing to mix until the dough comes together.
  4. Divide Dough: Gather the dough into a ball and divide it into two equal parts.
  5. Roll Out Dough: On a floured surface (or a silicon mat), flatten each portion with a rolling pin until it's wide enough to fit an 8 or 9-inch cake pan.
  6. Prepare Base Crust: Place one rolled-out dough into the cake pan to form the base. Set the second rolled-out dough aside; this will be the top crust.

Make the Filling:

  1. Heat Milk: Pour 1 can (300ml) of sweetened condensed milk into a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add Sugar: Stir in 2 tablespoons of sugar.
  3. Cook Coconut: Add 5-6 cups of young coconut meat slices and cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Thicken Filling: Pour in a mixture of ½ cup coconut water and ½ cup flour (diluted together), stirring thoroughly. Cook until the mixture thickens.
  5. Cool Filling: Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the filling cool down.

Assemble and Bake:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Fill the Crust: Pour the cooled filling into the crust-lined cake pan.
  3. Top Crust: Place the second piece of rolled-out dough over the filling and seal the edges.
  4. Create Vents: Poke holes in the top crust using a fork to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake: Bake for 45 to 55 minutes, or until the crust turns golden brown. Keep an eye on the color to ensure it's done.
  6. Cool and Serve: Let the pie cool before serving. Enjoy and share your delicious Buko Pie!

Espasol

Espasol is a traditional Filipino rice cake that hails from the province of Laguna. This sweet treat is particularly popular during the Christmas season. The main ingredients are rice flour, coconut milk, and sweetened coconut strips. These are cooked together until the mixture reaches a sticky consistency.

Once cooked, the mixture is shaped into long cylinders or triangles and coated with toasted rice flour, giving it a distinctive look and flavor. The combination of toasted glutinous rice flour and grated young coconut results in a texture that is soft, chewy, and pliable, making espasol a delightful and unique delicacy enjoyed by many.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of glutinous rice flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of coconut milk (gata)
  • 3/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of shredded young coconut (buko)

Procedures


  1. Toast the Rice Flour: In a wok, toast 2 cups of glutinous rice flour until it turns lightly brown. Let it cool, then set aside 1/2 cup for later use.
  2. Prepare the Coconut Milk Mixture: In a saucepan, combine 1 1/2 cups of coconut milk and 3/4 cup of sugar. Bring this mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  3. Add Flavorings and Buko: Add 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract and 1/4 teaspoon of salt to the saucepan. Stir in 2 cups of shredded young coconut (buko).
  4. Incorporate the Rice Flour: Add the toasted rice flour to the coconut mixture all at once. Stir continuously for about 15 minutes, until the mixture becomes very thick and dry.
  5. Shape the Mixture: Transfer the cooked mixture onto a baking sheet. Flatten it into a sheet about 1/4-inch thick, then cut it into cylinders. Alternatively, you can shape it by hand.
  6. Coat with Toasted Flour: Roll the cut cylinders in the reserved toasted rice flour.

Kesong Puti

Kesong puti is a traditional soft, white cheese from the Philippines, known for its creamy texture and distinctive flavor. Traditionally made from carabao milk, it is curdled using vinegar, citrus juices, or rennet, and seasoned with salt. Occasionally, kesong puti is also crafted from goat or cow milk, offering a slight variation in taste and texture.

This cheese has a mildly salty and tangy flavor, and its consistency can range from very moist to firm, depending on the preparation method. Similar in texture to queso blanco or buffalo mozzarella, kesong puti is versatile and can be enjoyed on its own, paired with bread such as pandesal, or incorporated into various Filipino dishes. Its rich, unique taste makes it a beloved delicacy in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of carabao milk
  • 3/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 cup of cane vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons of lemon juice
  • Banana leaves, cut into 12x6-inch pieces and passed over an open flame

Procedures


  1. Heat the Milk: In a double boiler over medium-low heat, combine 4 cups of carabao milk and 3/4 teaspoon of salt. Cook for 15 minutes, stirring continuously with a sterilized spoon to prevent the mixture from boiling.
  2. Form the Curds: Remove the milk from heat and add 1/4 cup of cane vinegar and 2 teaspoons of lemon juice. Stir lightly to allow curds to form, then let the mixture cool and set for 1 hour.
  3. Strain the Curds: Line a strainer with cheesecloth and place it over a mixing bowl. Once the mixture has cooled, pour it into the lined strainer to separate the liquid (whey) from the curds. Discard the liquid.
  4. For Creamy Kesong Puti: Use the curds as is.
  5. For Firmer Cheese: Place a clean, heavy object (like a small plate) on top of the curds to press out more whey.
  6. Wrap the Curds: Place the curds on a piece of banana leaf (cut into 12x6-inch pieces and passed over an open flame). Fold the leaf to wrap the curds and tie it with kitchen twine or a long strip of banana leaf.
  7. Store the Cheese: Place the wrapped curds in a zip-top bag or covered container and store in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

Puto

Puto, also known as Putong Bigas, is a beloved Filipino steamed rice cake made from rice dough called galapong. This treat, often referred to as putong puti due to its characteristic white color, is a staple in Filipino cuisine. Traditionally enjoyed as a merienda or snack, puto is one of the most popular types of kakanin—a category of Filipino delicacies made from glutinous rice, rice flour, and coconut.

Puto can be enjoyed plain, but it can also be enhanced with various toppings. A popular variation is cheese puto, which involves adding a slice of cheese on top before steaming, resulting in a simple yet delicious twist that many refer to as puto cheese. Whether enjoyed plain or with added flavors, puto remains a favorite among Filipinos for its light, fluffy texture and mildly sweet taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 1/4 tablespoons of baking powder
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of evaporated milk
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • 36 pieces of cheddar cheese

Procedures


  1. Prepare Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, sift together 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 cup of sugar, and 1 1/4 tablespoons of baking powder. Set aside.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, combine 1 cup of water, 1 cup of evaporated milk, 1 medium egg, and 1/4 cup of unsalted butter.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the wet ingredients. Mix the batter just until combined.
  4. Fill Muffin Tins: Spoon the batter into 1-ounce muffin tins until they are 3/4 full.
  5. Steam: Steam the puto for 10 minutes.
  6. Add Cheese Topping: After steaming for 10 minutes, place a piece of cubed cheese on top of each puto. Steam for an additional 1 minute, or until the cheese is slightly melted.
  7. Cool and Serve: Remove the puto from the steamer and allow them to cool. Serve and enjoy!

Monay Bae

Monay, derived from the Spanish Pan de Monja meaning "nun's bread," is a cherished Filipino bread with a rich history. This slightly sweet bun boasts a dense and heavy texture, featuring a characteristic deep partition that splits it into two distinct cheeks.

Often hailed as the "mother of all Filipino bread," monay serves as the foundational dough for other beloved bread varieties such as putok (star bread) and pinagong. While these breads share a similar dense texture, they each possess their own unique shapes achieved through distinctive scoring techniques. While monay is divided into two halves, putok showcases a cracked top, and pinagong is shaped like a turtle, adding to the diversity and charm of Filipino bread culture.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons baking powder
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/3 cup powdered skim milk
  • 1 cup and 3 tablespoons water
  • 1/8 cup margarine
  • extra margarine and white sugar, for topping

Procedures


  1. Take a large clean bowl and combine flour, yeast, salt, baking powder, brown sugar, and powdered skim milk, ensuring that the yeast and salt do not touch each other. Create a well in the center.
  2. Carefully pour water into the well.
  3. Stir with a spatula until the ingredients are fully combined and the dough appears ragged.
  4. Once the dough forms a rough mass, transfer it to a lightly floured surface and knead until it becomes smooth and glossy.
  5. Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a kitchen towel, and allow it to rise for about an hour or until it doubles in size.
  6. After the dough has risen, gently deflate it and transfer it to a lightly floured surface.
  7. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces and shape each piece into a tight ball. Arrange them on a baking tray, making an indentation on each ball with scissors.
  8. Cover the dough balls with a damp towel and let them rest (proof) for 1-1/2 hours.
  9. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  10. Once the dough has sufficiently proofed, bake it for 10 to 12 minutes or until it turns golden brown.
  11. After baking, brush the tops of the buns with margarine and sprinkle them with white sugar.

Bibingka Makapuno

Bibingka Macapuno is a delightful Filipino delicacy that combines the traditional bibingka—a type of rice cake—with macapuno, which are soft, jelly-like coconut strings. This dessert is known for its slightly chewy texture and mildly sweet taste, often enjoyed during the Christmas season and other festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • Macapuno
  • galapong
  • condensed milk
  • sugar
  • butter
  • pandan

Procedures


  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 ½ kg of crushed macapuno, water, pandan, and washed sugar. Stir the mixture well.
  2. Cover the saucepan and allow it to cook for 15 minutes.
  3. Once the mixture is cooked, add galapong and condensed milk, and mix thoroughly.
  4. Prepare a banana leaf by brushing it lightly with butter.
  5. Place the prepared banana leaf on a baking pan and pour the mixture onto it.
  6. Transfer the mixture to the oven and bake for 1 ½ hours. Then, serve.

Uraro

Uraro cookies, also known as galletas de liliw in Southern Luzon, are a delightful Filipino treat made from arrowroot flour, butter, and eggs. They have a soft, powdery texture that dissolves effortlessly in your mouth, making them a favorite snack in the region. These cookies come in various shapes like circles, stars, or flowers, adding to their charm. Enjoyed with a hot cup of coffee or tea, they make for a satisfying and comforting indulgence during any part of the day.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 cups arrowroot starch/flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Procedures


  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F.
  2. In a big bowl, sift together arrowroot flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Put aside.
  3. In another bowl, use an electric mixer to cream together the butter and sugar until it becomes creamy and fluffy.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Then mix in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the mixing bowl, starting at low speed to moisten them and then increasing the speed until everything is fully mixed.
  6. Beat just until the batter becomes smooth. Be careful not to overbeat.
  7. Transfer the batter into a cookie press. Squeeze the batter onto an ungreased baking sheet in single presses, leaving about 1 and ½ inches of space between each cookie.
  8. Bake in the preheated 350°F oven for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the undersides start to turn golden.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool completely in the pan. To remove them, gently slide a cookie with your fingers until it releases. Avoid using a spatula, as the cookies are delicate and might break.
