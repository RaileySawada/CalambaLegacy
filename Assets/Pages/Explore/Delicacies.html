Delicacies

Delicacies typically refer to rare, luxurious, or exquisite foods that are considered especially pleasing or refined. These can include dishes made from unique ingredients, prepared with exceptional skill, or embodying cultural significance, often reserved for special occasions or esteemed gatherings. Delicacies are often prized for their flavors, textures, and the experience they offer to those who indulge in them.

Buko Pie

Buko Pie is a super yummy dessert! It's made with soft coconut and a creamy filling inside a crispy, buttery crust. It's a popular snack or after-dinner treat in the Philippines!

People often bring it home as a gift, especially from Laguna, where it first started. Colette's and Lety's shops are really famous for selling this tasty pie, but they didn't invent it.

Espasol

Espasol is a yummy rice cake made with rice flour and coconut milk. It comes from Laguna and you can find it at bus stops and gift shops.

Just like other popular rice treats, there are lots of ways to make Espasol. I'll share a simple recipe with you. Say goodbye to your cravings and let's make some delicious Espasol!

Kesong Puti

Kesong puti is a soft, white cheese from the Philippines. It's made from carabao milk and salt, and it's curdled with vinegar, citrus juices, or rennet. Sometimes, it's made from goat or cow milk. It tastes a little salty and tangy. Depending on how it's made, it can be like queso blanco or buffalo mozzarella. It can be very moist or firm. People eat it alone, with bread like pandesal, or in different Filipino dishes.

Puto

Puto, also known as Putong Bigas, is a classic Filipino steamed rice cake made from rice dough called galapong. It's often called putong puti, meaning "white puto," because it's very white in color. It's one of the most loved types of puto in the Philippines.

Monay Bae

Bay's monay stands out among other bread varieties because it's bigger and less sweet. Its crust is smooth and doesn't have any glaze, and it contains a few raisins that add unique flavors. Each bite is delicious and unmatched. If you're interested in trying it, you can also buy it at Tenorio's Bakery on Rizal Avenue in Bay Laguna.

Bibingka Makapuno

Bibingka Macapuno is a delightful Filipino delicacy that combines the traditional bibingka—a type of rice cake—with macapuno, which are soft, jelly-like coconut strings. This dessert is known for its slightly chewy texture and mildly sweet taste, often enjoyed during the Christmas season and other festive occasions.

Uraro

Uraro cookies are a Filipino delicacy crafted from arrowroot flour, butter, and eggs. These treats melt in your mouth and are ideal for a quick snack during the day. They pair wonderfully with a cup of coffee or tea!

In Southern Luzon, especially in the region of Liliw, these cookies are known as galletas de liliw and are a local specialty. They have a soft, powdery texture that dissolves effortlessly and a rich, buttery flavor. Typically, they come in different shapes like circles, stars, or flowers.
